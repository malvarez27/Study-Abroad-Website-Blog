Academics Offered

THST 3200 : Prophecy and Society in New Zealand

Professor Daniel Smith-Christopher

4 Units

*This course satisfies the LMU core requirement for Integrations: Faith and Reason as well as the Flag for Engaged Learning.*

In New Zealand history, the missionary impact on the indigenous people of New Zealand, the Maori, was immense. The Maori adopted Christianity in large numbers in the 19th Century, but began to interpret and live it in their own unique manner. In this course, we will examine the particular impact of the Biblical Prophets of the Old Testament on the Maori. The 19th Century saw the rise of Maori Prophets who became significant indigenous leaders.

ASPA 4998 : History and Culture of New Zealand

Professor Daniel Smith-Christopher

4 Units

New Zealand is among the most vibrant countries and cross-cultural societies in the Pacific Rim. A stunning country visually, it is also fascinating culturally, historically, and artistically. In this course, we will survey New Zealand history, New Zealand art and popular culture, and visit major sites in the country, including Te Papa and the Parliament Buildings (Wellington), The Auckland Museum, Waitangi Treaty Grounds, Bay of Islands, KeriKeri and the Stone House, Rotorua, and many more cultural and historical sites. New Zealand guest lecturers will provide most of the exciting content of this course.

New Zealand

Summer of 2018 I had the chance to study abroad for a month in New Zealand. New Zealand is a very unique country to visit. It is known for their Maori culture, manuka honey, rugby, etc. Oh and did I mention.. SHEEP! I had an amazing time living in New Zealand in the summer. I cannot wait to visit again soon.

Week 1 Cultural Experience : Gisborne

Gisborne was out first stop in New Zealand. It was a cultural Experience that I will never forget. We arrived and began learning about the M훮ori culture right away. We were greeted and taken to a school where we were fed and had a lecture. We quickly had to learn a song titled T큰tira Mai Ng훮 Iwi which is the M훮ori folk song. After, the first day we drove to a Maori Marae or wharenui (meeting house) which is where we experienced and taking part in a powhiri (formal welcome ceremony) which is a must-do during your stay. The six main steps of a powhiri are the following; karanga (the call of welcome), individual from the group replies to the karanga by guiding the visitor group to walk forward, formal speeches (whaikorero), waiata (song), gift (usually a monetary) called a koha, lastly hosts line up and the guests with the men in front walk forward to press noses, called the hongi (traditional greeting called a hariru). We stayed at several Marae's during our stay in Gisborne which was a one of a king experience that I might never have again.

Weekend 1 Excursion : Wellington

Wellington is the capital city of New Zealand. It is very far south of the North island. Wellington is known for Cuba street, cable car museum, all of the shops, restaurants, parks, and the Te Papa museum.In Wellington, we had a city tour where we were able to view all the local spots. We had a guided tour of the New Zealand Parliament where we where saw the Banquet Hall, Parliamentary House,Debating Chamber, Legislative Council Chamber (LCC), Grand Hall, Parliamentary Library, Walk through the Galleria, Select Committee Rooms, and The Base Isolators. We also had a tour of the Weta Cave Workshop, which is a world-leading desing and effects facility. They have worked on many famous films like; The Lord of the Rings, MEG, Hobbit, The Amazing Spider- Man 2, Avatar, and so much more! Our excursion in Wellington was one to see!

Weekend 2 Excursion : Bay of Islands

The Bay of Islands was a magical place. There is a small local town where we stayed with delicious food and cute boutiques. We went on a boat trip, viewed the first European building (the stone building), and visited where the Waitangi Grounds. The Waitangi grounds is where the Treaty of Waitangi was signed.

Weekend 4 : Rotorua

Rotorua is a city like no other. In Rotorua we visited Te Puia, Agrodome, and Skyline Rotorua. Te Puia is a Maori cultural center at a geothermal preserve with a dramatic geyser & crafts institute.
